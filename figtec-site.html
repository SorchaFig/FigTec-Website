The Problem

Coercive control is an epidemic
without a scalable solution.

Coercive and controlling behaviour is one of the most damaging and least understood forms of domestic abuse. Unlike physical violence, it operates invisibly: isolating victims, eroding identity, and making escape feel impossible.

Survivors face fragmented, underfunded, and geographically limited support systems. Crisis services are overstretched. Waiting lists are long. And existing digital tools were never designed with trauma in mind.

Fig™ changes that. By combining clinical best practice with intuitive, compassionate technology, Fig™ meets survivors exactly where they are: on their phone, at any hour, without judgment.

The Founder

Engineer. Analyst. Elected leader. Advocate.

Sorcha McAnespy is the founder of FigTec Ltd and brings over a decade of experience across public service, political leadership, and systems-level advocacy. She is an engineer and data analyst whose career has always centred on making institutions work better for the people they serve.

A member of Fianna Fail for over ten years, Sorcha has been elected to the party's Ard Chomhairle and is a respected voice in Irish political life. As an elected councillor on Fermanagh and Omagh District Council, she served as Chair of the inaugural Policing and Community Safety Partnership (PCSP) and Chair of the Western Trust Local Commissioning Group, working across statutory services, community organisations, and cross-border stakeholders.

Her approach to coercive control is rooted in governance, health commissioning, and policy. She is particularly focused on reframing coercive control as a pattern-based, systemic harm that requires earlier recognition and preventative responses across justice, health, education, and workplaces. She is a frequent speaker on coercive control, public safety, and the role of technology in reducing harm before crisis point.
